# Brainstorming Session Results

**Facilitator:** Juan
**Date:** 2026-04-30 21:02:07

## Session Overview

**Topic:** Quick stock-health indicators and analyst-style trend interpretation
**Goals:** Generate user-facing indicators that help someone decide quickly whether a stock is doing well or badly, and identify better ways to turn already calculated data into trend and decision-support signals.

### Session Setup

This session was run as a direct ideation sprint rather than a gated workshop. The working definition of "doing good or bad" was reduced to four practical questions: Is price trend positive, is that trend trustworthy, is the move supported by participation, and is the setup improving or deteriorating relative to alternatives.

## Technique Selection

**Approach:** AI-Recommended Techniques
**Analysis Context:** quick-glance stock judgment with stronger use of derived analytics

**Recommended Techniques:**

- **First Principles Thinking:** Reduce "good or bad" into trend, confirmation, risk, and context instead of piling on raw indicators.
- **Six Thinking Hats:** Force ideas across factual, risk, opportunity, process, and narrative lenses so the output is not just momentum-heavy.
- **Analogical Thinking:** Borrow from dashboards, diagnostics, weather maps, and analyst notes to create glanceable indicators instead of spreadsheet fragments.
- **Reverse Brainstorming:** Identify where indicators can mislead users so the final concepts include anti-signals and trust markers.

**AI Rationale:** The problem is not a lack of numbers. It is a translation gap between calculated data and a fast human decision. These techniques fit because they combine reduction, interpretation, explainability, and skepticism.

## Technique Execution Results

**Interactive Focus:** Direct ideation across 10 deliberately different domains, pivoting every 10 ideas to avoid semantic clustering.
**Key Breakthroughs:** The strongest concepts were not single metrics. They were compact verdict systems that combine state, trust, change, and relative context.

### Idea Inventory

#### Domain 1: Instant Price Health

**[Category #1]**: Trend Traffic Light
_Concept_: Collapse price health into green, yellow, or red using price location versus 20, 50, and 200-day trend lines plus current slope direction. This becomes the first answer to "is it acting well right now?"
_Novelty_: It judges structure and direction together instead of treating moving-average crosses as isolated events.

**[Category #2]**: Distance From Trend Spine
_Concept_: Show how far price sits from a blended trend spine made from short, medium, and long averages. Healthy trends stay stretched but not absurd; unhealthy moves are either broken below or too euphoric above.
_Novelty_: It reframes extension as a health signal rather than only a momentum signal.

**[Category #3]**: Breakout Integrity Badge
_Concept_: Mark recent breakouts as clean, shaky, or failed based on follow-through days, close quality, and whether the stock held the breakout zone. Users quickly see whether a breakout is real or already degrading.
_Novelty_: It focuses on the quality of post-breakout behavior, not just the breakout event itself.

**[Category #4]**: Drawdown Thermometer
_Concept_: Display current drawdown from the recent high alongside recovery speed from the last swing low. A stock can be down a lot but healing fast, or near highs but quietly weakening.
_Novelty_: It puts damage and repair on the same scale.

**[Category #5]**: Closing Strength Meter
_Concept_: Measure where the stock closes inside its daily range over a rolling window and summarize whether buyers are consistently winning the close. Repeated weak closes signal distribution even when the chart still looks acceptable.
_Novelty_: It uses micro-structure to assess underlying conviction.

**[Category #6]**: Gap Quality Stamp
_Concept_: Classify gaps as escape, continuation, noise, or exhaustion using gap size, volume, and next-day hold behavior. Users stop overreacting to every open jump.
_Novelty_: It teaches users that not all gaps deserve the same interpretation.

**[Category #7]**: Trend Staircase
_Concept_: Count recent higher highs and higher lows, then render them as visible steps. A strong staircase means the trend is doing the hard work correctly even if momentum oscillators look ordinary.
_Novelty_: It turns raw swing structure into a human-readable pattern score.

**[Category #8]**: Regime Flip Alert
_Concept_: Detect when price behavior changes from expansion to chop, or from chop to trend, using volatility compression, slope, and support retention. This alerts users when an old mental model is no longer valid.
_Novelty_: It focuses on state transition rather than current static label.

**[Category #9]**: Time-in-Strength Gauge
_Concept_: Show what percentage of recent days price spent above its key trend anchor and how that ratio is changing. Sustained time in strength matters more than a single dramatic up day.
_Novelty_: It tracks persistence, which users rarely get from standard charts.

**[Category #10]**: Price Health Sentence
_Concept_: Generate a one-line summary such as "Uptrend intact, slightly extended, still well supported above medium trend." This gives the user an analyst-style read before they parse visuals.
_Novelty_: It converts quantitative state into plain-language judgment.

#### Domain 2: Trend Quality And Analyst-Style Derived Signals

**[Category #11]**: Slope Stack
_Concept_: Compare short, medium, and long trend slopes in one stack and label them aligned, mixed, or deteriorating. Users immediately see whether the trend is broad or only short-term.
_Novelty_: It makes multi-horizon agreement the center of the story.

**[Category #12]**: Trend Acceleration Pulse
_Concept_: Separate positive slope from accelerating slope so users can tell whether the trend is strengthening or merely still positive. This distinguishes maturing winners from emerging leaders.
_Novelty_: It treats second derivative as a first-class indicator for decision speed.

**[Category #13]**: Trend Consistency Score
_Concept_: Score how smooth the advance has been by comparing directional progress to path noise. Trends with steady progress usually deserve more trust than equally strong but erratic moves.
_Novelty_: It measures how expensive the trend is emotionally to hold.

**[Category #14]**: Multi-Horizon Agreement Ribbon
_Concept_: Show whether the 1-week, 1-month, 3-month, and 6-month trends agree or conflict. A mixed ribbon warns that a user may be borrowing conviction from the wrong timeframe.
_Novelty_: It prevents short-term strength from masquerading as durable health.

**[Category #15]**: Analyst Trend Summary
_Concept_: Produce a concise note like "Long trend still constructive, short trend improving after a messy base, confirmation incomplete." This uses existing derived values to sound more like a human analyst.
_Novelty_: It elevates interpretation above raw display.

**[Category #16]**: Inflection Detector
_Concept_: Flag when deterioration slows before price visibly recovers, or when strength stalls before breakdown becomes obvious. This gives users early notice that the trajectory itself is changing.
_Novelty_: It hunts turning behavior rather than waiting for a full reversal.

**[Category #17]**: Trend Half-Life
_Concept_: Estimate how quickly recent strength decays when buying pressure fades by studying the persistence of prior bursts. This helps users judge whether momentum historically sticks or evaporates.
_Novelty_: It treats trend durability as a measurable trait.

**[Category #18]**: Momentum Fatigue Signal
_Concept_: Highlight when price still rises but acceleration, closing strength, and relative strength begin fading together. The user sees "still up" and "less healthy" as separate states.
_Novelty_: It formalizes the difference between trend presence and trend quality.

**[Category #19]**: Smooth Versus Choppy Ratio
_Concept_: Measure how much of recent movement came from orderly directional travel versus violent reversals. This tells the user whether the stock is rewarding patience or demanding heroic timing.
_Novelty_: It approximates behavioral difficulty, not just technical direction.

**[Category #20]**: If-This-Continues Path
_Concept_: Project a near-term path band based on current slope, volatility, and consistency, then phrase it as "If current conditions persist, price behavior likely remains constructive unless support X breaks."
_Novelty_: It offers conditional analyst framing instead of false certainty.

#### Domain 3: Volume And Participation

**[Category #21]**: Volume Confirmation Halo

**[Category #22]**: Accumulation Distribution Pulse

**[Category #23]**: Quiet Strength Detector
_Concept_: Flag stocks rising steadily on ordinary volume with strong close behavior and low churn. Some of the best moves are supported by calm demand, not fireworks.
_Novelty_: It prevents the interface from over-rewarding only dramatic volume spikes.

**[Category #24]**: Exhaustion Spike Alert
_Concept_: Mark outsized volume spikes that occur after extended runs and poor closes as possible exhaustion instead of celebration. This protects users from confusing climax with confirmation.
_Novelty_: It applies skepticism to the same signal many dashboards glorify.

**[Category #25]**: Sponsor Quality Proxy
_Concept_: Infer whether buying looks institutional or retail-like using trade persistence, range control, and follow-through. The idea is to label participation as sticky or fragile.
_Novelty_: It tries to translate tape character into a trust judgment.

**[Category #26]**: Volume At Price Acceptance
_Concept_: Show where heavy recent trading built real acceptance and where price simply passed through. Users can see whether the current zone has structural support beneath it.
_Novelty_: It upgrades support from visual guesswork to evidence-backed acceptance.

**[Category #27]**: Up-Day Participation Share
_Concept_: Track how much of recent total volume happened on positive days. A strong stock often attracts more committed participation on its up moves than on its down moves.
_Novelty_: It normalizes participation quality across a full window rather than one day.

**[Category #28]**: Breakout Support Monitor
_Concept_: After a breakout, watch whether supportive volume appears on retests and dries up on pullbacks. This helps distinguish healthy digestion from abandonment.
_Novelty_: It treats volume as a post-event trust monitor, not just a trigger.

**[Category #29]**: Liquidity Stress Warning
_Concept_: Warn when apparent strength happens in conditions too thin to trust, such as light average volume or widening ranges. The stock may be moving, but not in a way users can rely on.
_Novelty_: It inserts execution realism into bullish chart reading.

**[Category #30]**: Participation Narrative
_Concept_: Generate plain-English notes like "Recent gains are supported by steady accumulation, not just one headline spike." This makes volume behavior usable for fast decision-making.
_Novelty_: It turns a specialized pane into a readable verdict.

**[Category #40]**: Watchlist Percentile

### Session Highlights
